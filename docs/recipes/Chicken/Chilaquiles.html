Chilaquiles

  1. 1 Tbsp canola oil
  2. 1 lb. ground turkey
  3. 2 red onions
  4. 4 cloves garlic
  5. 1/4 tsp cumin
  6. 1 1/2 tsp chili powder
  7. 4 oz. can diced green chilies, not drained
  8. 1 tsp pepper, salt
  9. 15 oz. can tomatoes with with green chilies, not drained
  10. 2 cups cheddar
  11. 1 8 oz light cream cheese
  12. 8 oz. sour cream
  13. 8 (6”) tortillas
  14. 1 bunch green onions
  15. 1/4 cups low fat mozzarella cheese
  • Heat oven to 350.
  • Heat a large nonstick skillet over medium high heat.
  • Add 1 Tbsp canola oil to coat bottom of the pot.
  • Add the ground turkey to the hot oil and brown, breaking it up as it cooks.
  • Drain meat.
  • To the same skillet which has a little residual fat from cooking the turkey, add the onions and cook over medium heat until soft; then add the garlic and cook until soft.
  • Stir in the chili powder and cumin.
  • Add the chilies and their juices and the tomatoes and their juices.
  • Add the meat back to the pan and stir until combined.
  • Reduce heat to low and simmer for 20 minutes or until thick.
  • Season with salt and pepper.
  • In a medium bowl combine cheddar, cream cheese and sour cream; set aside.
  • In a casserole dish line with half the tortillas and ladle half of the meat mix over.
  • Cover with half of the cheese mix (spread).
  • Top with half of the green onions.
  • Repeat layers 1 more time but leave off the rest of the green onions until later.
  • Bake for 45 minutes or until bubbling.
  • Top with green onions and mozzarella.
  • Cook a few more minutes.
  • Can skip the mozzarella--I didn’t have any the first time I made it, and it was fine.
