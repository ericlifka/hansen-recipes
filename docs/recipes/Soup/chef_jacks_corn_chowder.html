Chef Jack’s Corn Chowder

  • 1 cups butter
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cups flour
  • 3 cups corn kernels, fresh or frozen
  • 3 cups chicken stock
  • 2 cups half and half
  1. Melt 1 stick butter in pan over medium heat.
  2. Add onion, carrot, celery and garlic and sauté for 2 minutes.
  3. Add the flour and stir to make a roux.
  4. Cook until the roux is lightly browned, set aside to cool.
  5. Meanwhile, combine corn and stock in another pan and bring to a boil.
  6. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  7. Pour the boiling stock with the corn (a little at a time) into the pan with the roux, whisking briskly so it doesn’t lump.
  8. Return the skillet to the heat and bring to a boil.
  9. The mix should become thick.
  10. Add half and half into the corn mix.
  11. Just before serving, cut the remaining stick of butter into chunks and add to soup.
  12. I generally don’t add the other stick of butter because of all the fat.
  13. I also frequently save left over corn from dinners in a baggie and freeze and use that for the corn.
