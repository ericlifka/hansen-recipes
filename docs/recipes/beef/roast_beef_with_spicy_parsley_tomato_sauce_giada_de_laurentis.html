Roast Beef with Spicy Parsley Tomato Sauce (Giada De Laurentis)

  • 2 to 2 1/2 lb. sirloin tip or chuck roast
  • 3 Tbsp olive oil
  • 4 Roma tomatoes, cut in half
  • 2 tsp herbs de provence
  • kosher salt and pepper
  • Spicy Parsley Tomato Sauce:
  • 1 1/2 cups flat leaf parsley
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 3/4 tsp kosher salt
  • 3/4 tsp pepper
  • 2 Tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1/2 cups olive oil
  1. Heat oven to 375.
  2. Season the beef with salt and pepper.
  3. Season the tomatoes with salt, pepper and herbs de provence.
  4. Place a heavy roasting pan over high heat.
  5. Heat the oil oil.
  6. Sear the beef over high heat on all sides.
  7. turn off heat.
  8. Place the seasoned tomatoes around the seared beef and place the pan in the oven.
  9. I put some beef broth in the bottom of the pan to prevent sticking.
  10. Roast until meat thermometer reads 130 for medium rare, 135 of medium about 30-35 minutes.
  11. Take the roast out of the oven, tent loosely with foil and let rest for 10-15 minutes.
  12. The internal temperature of the meat will rise 5 degrees more.
  13. I did it at 350 for 1 1/2 hours.
  14. To make the sauce, place the parsley and garlic in a food processor and pulse until the parsley is finely chopped.
  15. Add the red pepper flakes, salt, red wine vinegar and the roasted tomatoes from the beef pan and process until pureed.
  16. Add the olive oil in a steady stream with the machine running.
  17. To serve, slice the roast and place on a serving platter.
  18. Drizzle a little sauce over the meat.
  19. Serve the remaining sauce in a small bowl alongside.
